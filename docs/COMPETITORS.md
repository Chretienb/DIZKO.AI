# Dizko.ai — Competitive Landscape & Positioning

## One-line positioning
> **Dizko.ai is the AI-native home for collaborative music production** — it
> auto-organizes and analyzes your stems, generates mixes, and turns a story post
> into new collaborators. Most tools do *one* of: cloud storage, a browser DAW, or
> sample libraries. Dizko ties **AI stem intelligence + crew collaboration +
> discovery** together.

---

## The landscape (who we're up against)

### A. Cloud collaboration / project sync
| Tool | What it is | Where Dizko wins |
|---|---|---|
| **Splice (Studio)** | Sample library + project backup/versioning | Splice is sample-first; Dizko is *stem-intelligence* first (auto BPM/key/naming, Smart Mix) and built around a crew, not solo backups. |
| **Satellite Sessions** | DAW-agnostic cloud sync of project files | Satellite syncs files between DAWs; Dizko needs **no DAW** and adds AI analysis + in-browser review + discovery. |
| **Avid Cloud Collaboration** | Pro Tools cloud sessions | Locked to Pro Tools and pros; Dizko is browser-based and approachable. |
| **Audiomovers / Soundwhale** | Real-time audio streaming for remote sessions | They stream live monitoring; Dizko owns the **async** workflow (organize, comment, version, mix). |

### B. Browser DAWs / social creation
| Tool | What it is | Where Dizko wins |
|---|---|---|
| **BandLab** | Free social browser DAW + huge community | BandLab is a full DAW + feed; Dizko isn't trying to be a DAW — it's the **stem workspace + AI layer** that sits around whatever DAW you use, with role-based pro collaboration. |
| **Soundtrap (Spotify)** | Browser DAW, collab, education | Education/consumer leaning; Dizko targets serious creators shipping releases. |
| **Endlesss (defunct)** | Collaborative live jamming | Validated demand for collab; Dizko is the durable, project-based take. |

### C. Sharing / feedback / A&R
| Tool | What it is | Where Dizko wins |
|---|---|---|
| **Disco.ac / Highnote** | Private streaming + feedback for labels/A&R | They share *finished* music for review; Dizko is upstream — where the music is **made and assembled** by the crew. |
| **Blend (defunct)** | Stem-sharing community | Dizko brings that "share your stems, find collaborators" energy back, with AI + a real product around it. |

### D. AI audio tools
| Tool | What it is | Where Dizko wins |
|---|---|---|
| **LANDR** | AI mastering + samples + distribution | LANDR is point-tools stitched together; Dizko makes AI native to the **collaborative project** (naming, analysis, separation, Smart Mix in context). |
| **iZotope / standalone stem splitters** | Single-purpose AI processors | Dizko folds analysis & separation into the team workflow instead of one-off uploads. |

## Why Dizko is different (the wedge)

1. **AI does the grunt work, in context.** Upload raw, messy files → get named,
   key/BPM-tagged, mix-ready stems automatically. Competitors make you organize;
   Dizko organizes for you.
2. **Stem-centric, crew-centric.** Roles, per-stem comments, versions/takes,
   approvals, and presence — a real production workflow, not a file locker or a
   solo DAW.
3. **Built-in discovery via the share card.** A faded-Polaroid + QR you post to
   your story drops scanners into a "request to join" flow. No other tool turns a
   social post into a vetted collaborator that cleanly.
4. **Owner-pays growth model.** Collaborators join free and contribute; only
   creation/output is paid. This is the same loop that made Figma and Google Docs
   spread — applied to music.
5. **No DAW required.** Everything (upload, analyze, review, mix, export) happens
   in the browser; finish in any DAW via export.

## Positioning statement (for pitches)
> For **artists and producers who make music with other people**, **Dizko.ai** is
> the **AI-native collaboration workspace** that **turns raw stems into organized,
> analyzed, mix-ready projects and finds you collaborators** — unlike **sample
> libraries, browser DAWs, or file-sync tools**, which each solve only one slice
> and leave the AI organization, crew workflow, and discovery to you.

## Risks / where competitors could push back
- **BandLab / Soundtrap** could bolt on AI organization — but they're anchored to
  being full DAWs and broad communities, not the focused stem+crew workflow.
- **Splice** could lean further into collaboration — but its identity and revenue
  are sample subscriptions.
- **Moats to build:** the AI quality (naming/analysis/Smart Mix), the
  collaborator graph (who's worked with whom), and the discovery loop (share-card
  network effects).
